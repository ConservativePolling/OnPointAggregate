Introducing OnPointAggregator AI

Now rolling out across OnPointAggregate — a neural assistant that accesses the latest site data, visualizes aggregates, extracts trend information, and more. Watch the presentation below, then scroll the article to play each chapter.

Introducing OnPointAggregator AI — our new AI integration for OnPointAggregate — now rolling out and soon available to all. Built to be fast, precise, and transparent, it works alongside the aggregate to help you explore trends, validate narratives, and generate usable summaries with citations.

It accesses the latest site data, visualizes aggregates, extracts trend information, and more.

OnPointAggregator AI pulls directly from the same JSON feeds that power our aggregate. It fetches the latest polls, merges them by series, and respects each pollster’s weight. When you ask for a timeframe (say, the last 20 days), it reasons about the window against the current index and only includes entries that fall inside that range. If you zoom out, it adjusts smoothing and tick density so the picture stays legible.

It can also add plain‑language context around what changed. If a sudden shift appears, the assistant will look for nearby events — releases, debates, major news — and describe plausible drivers while clearly separating facts from interpretation. When sources are available, it links them inline.

The assistant lives in the lower‑right corner of your screen. Tap it when you want a quick read, a chart, or a sanity check. It stays out of the way, but it’s always close enough to keep you moving.

Feedback welcome. We will keep tuning speed, retrieval coverage, and visualization polish as we roll OnPointAggregator AI.

Speed is the feature. When you ask a question, you should get a clear, legible answer quickly — without sacrificing grounding in the underlying data. Type, send, read. No labyrinth of menus. The assistant stays in the same frame as the plot so you can scan the visual and the words in one pass.

In practice, that means aggressive caching,better rendering, and small but deliberate animations that never get in your way. It also means being opinionated about defaults: we surface the most likely view first and give you just enough control to nudge it, not rebuild it.

OnPointAggregator AI plugs directly into the aggregate you already use. In this next segment, we put it to work on a focused question, surface the right plot, and keep the context in view so it feels effortless to iterate.

Usefulness is about surfacing the right details at the right moment. Beyond charts, the assistant can explain the timeframe used, list which polls contributed, and call out outliers or methodological notes when they meaningfully change the read. Citations stay one click away so you can verify as you go.
